### 1.1 "Easter Island: The First Three Expeditions" (modern compilation using Corney translations) (7 files)

This is a modern compilation titled "Easter Island: The First Three Expeditions" that draws on Bolton Glanvill Corney (ed.), *The Voyage of Captain Don Felipe Gonzalez to Easter Island in 1770-1, Preceded by an Extract from Mynheer Jacob Roggeveen's Official Log of his Discovery of and Visit to Easter Island in 1722* (Hakluyt Society, Second Series, No. XIII. London, 1903). The compilation uses Corney's translations but adds modern narrative commentary (e.g., "Dutch sailors spotted an unknown land" is modern prose, not from the Hakluyt Society volume).

**Important**: The compilation covers all three expeditions as its title implies, but with different types of content. Roggeveen (1722) and Gonzalez (1770) are represented by translated primary sources drawn from Corney 1903. Cook (1774) is covered by a modern narrative summary (pp. 99-111 of Part 3) that is NOT from Corney and NOT listed in the compilation's INDEX. The INDEX visible in all three Parts lists only Roggeveen and Gonzalez entries, ending at p. 79. The Cook section appears after the Gonzalez material (which ends at p. 94) without an index entry. The Corney 1903 Hakluyt Society volume itself does not include Cook material; Cook's presence in the compilation is entirely editorial addition.

Parts 1-3 are not direct scans of the Hakluyt Society volume but a modern repackaging with added narrative commentary.

### Sources Present (22 unique files)

1. "Easter Island: The First Three Expeditions" in 3 parts (modern compilation using Corney's translations of Roggeveen and Gonzalez primary accounts, plus a modern narrative summary of Cook's 1774 visit in Part 3 pp. 99-111 that is not from Corney)
2. Cook 1846 compilation (all three Cook voyages, including 1774 Easter Island)
3. Two La Perouse Easter Island chapter extracts (both from the Da Capo/Bibliotheca Australiana facsimile reprint, near-duplicates with slightly different page ranges)
4. Richards 2008 complete (post-La Perouse visits 1793-1861)
5. Von Saher: 8 articles covering Roggeveen, Bouman, and Forster journals
6. Jakubowska: 5 works covering Behrens, Forster manuscript, and Spanish expedition documents
7. Leendart Roggeveen: 2 articles on Roggeveen expedition wreck archaeology

### Notable Gaps

**High priority** (would strengthen the collection):
- Behrens, Carl Friedrich. *Der wohlversuchte Sudlander* (1737) or English translation. We have Jakubowska's analysis of two editions but not the text itself. This is one of three independent primary accounts from 1722.
- Mellen Blanco, Francisco. 1986. *Manuscritos y documentos espanoles para la historia de la isla de Pascua*. Madrid: CEHOPU. The Spanish-language originals of the Gonzalez expedition documents that Corney translated. Jakubowska 2014 discusses discrepancies in Corney's rendition.

**Medium priority** (useful but possibly redundant with what we have):
- Cook, James. 1777. *A Voyage Towards the South Pole and Round the World* (the official Second Voyage account). We have the 1846 compilation, but Cook's own 1777 text would be the authoritative primary source.
- Forster, Georg. 1777. *A Voyage Round the World*. Published six weeks before Cook's account. Includes detailed Easter Island observations.
- Forster, Johann Reinhold. 1778. *Observations Made During a Voyage Round the World*. Analytical and theoretical companion to the narrative accounts.

**Lower priority** (supplementary):
- Rollin, M. "Dissertation on the inhabitants of Easter Island." La Perouse's ship surgeon; medical/ethnographic observations.
- Lesseps, Jean-Baptiste Barthelemy de. 1790. *Journal historique du voyage de M. de Lesseps*. Overland journey from Kamchatka carrying La Perouse's records.
- Individual ship logs: Kotzebue (1821), Beechey (1825), etc. Richards 2008 likely covers these adequately.
