The following chart lists known ship arrivals to Easter Island 
from 1722-1879. This clearly shows that Rapa Nui was not so 
isolated in historic times as might be imagined. Where the infor-
mation is available, we have indicated which were whalers, but 
many more probably were in that category also. While few ships 
spent much time on the island, there was trading going on, and 
contact with the islanders was occurring, at various levels. Many 
ships "sighted" the island but either chose not to land, or were 
unable to land due to high surf or hostile gestures from the Ra-
panui . We chose to stop the list at 1879 when there is one last 
mention of Roussel returning to the island. Our thanks to Grant 
McCall for permission to use this portion of his publication.
