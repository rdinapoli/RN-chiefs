# Source Summary: Roggeveen 1722

## Overview

The Dutch expedition under Commander Jacob Roggeveen visited Rapa Nui on April 5-12, 1722 (one day ashore, April 10). Three ships, ~223 crew, ~134 armed men landed. This was the first European contact. A shooting incident killed 10-12 islanders shortly after landing.

## Sources Consulted

- Roggeveen's official log (Corney 1903 translation, via "Easter Island Expeditions" Part 1)
- Bouman's journal (von Saher 1994 full translation)
- Von Saher 1990a, 1990b, 1993 (additional journal details)
- Von Saher 1993b (inventory of all narratives, reliability assessment)
- Jakubowska 2012 (analysis of Behrens' two editions)

## Key Findings for Political Authority

**One substantive observation of authority**: A feathered individual in distinctive white dress (feathers, garment, shell) directed provisioning after the shooting. Bouman describes a specific threat-and-submission sequence: the individual ordered someone who refused, threatened with a stone, the person prostrated, then complied. Roggeveen describes the same person as "a particular one who seemed to be in authority over the other headmen" whose orders were "promptly obeyed with reverence and bowing."

**Critical context**: This occurred after the traumatic shooting, in the specific context of mediating relations with armed foreigners who had just killed islanders. Whether this individual's authority extended beyond this crisis situation is unknown.

**No other evidence of political hierarchy**: No organized reception, no tribute or redistribution, no hereditary rank information, no territorial control described, no weapons observed, no differential wealth, no formal religious authority. Exchange was individual and opportunistic. Crowd behavior was undirected. The first islander to visit the ships was a naked man in a small canoe, not a delegation.

**European categories imposed**: "King or Head Chief" (Roggeveen, once), "headmen" (Roggeveen), "chiefs" (Bouman, plural). All are European terms imposed on observed behavior. Behrens' narrative is unreliable (exaggerations, contradictions with Roggeveen and Bouman).

**Reliability hierarchy** (von Saher 1993b): Roggeveen journal and Bouman journal are the only reliable primary sources. Anonymous narratives (1727, 1728) and Behrens (1737) are derivative and unreliable.

## Relevance to the Paper

The 1722 accounts provide the thinnest evidentiary basis of any major contact-era visit (one day, one location, crisis conditions after a shooting). The single observation of directive authority has been used to support chiefdom models, but its context (post-shooting crisis mediation with armed foreigners) makes it inadequate as evidence for institutional hereditary chieftainship.
